I've always kind of gotten this impression, too.  It befuddles me to say the
least.  Personally, I really dig it.  I mean, it has some great tracks.  "A
Letter," "Nevermore," "Split To The East," "Blood & Bone," etc.  Really, I
like the whole album.  I love listening to it while driving.  It and Living
In A Moon So Blue are mainly worthwhile, I think, because they create an
extremely trance-like atmosphere.  I find it extremely easy to get lost in.
The constant stream of short, similar sounding songs works extremely well.

But that's just what I think.  I'm also a huge fan of Nine Thirty and Your
Turn To Fall, both of which I think are often underrated and ignored.
