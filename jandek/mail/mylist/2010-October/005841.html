Well, "Mark Greenberg" (if that is your real name), I don't to agree to disagree on Chusid's intent. People write books to share knowledge or to entertain. They sell them to make money.  That's really all there is to it. I'm not sure why you're embarrassed to point out that Jandek sells his CDs for money. I don't even understand what that has to do with what we've been discussing. My initial point was that once I pay for my copy of Chusid's book, I don't own him anything. Once I pay Corwood for a CD I don't own them anything either. They both sell their products to make money. Why else would someone sell something? Whether they do it to make profit is another story.  So what exactly are you implying? That Chusid wrote his book because it was that important to him to let the world know that Jandek is some kind of deranged lunatic?  I think that the money reason actually sounds better for him. 

I think that it's admirable in a way that you want to defend your buddy but the fact that you know him personally actually weakens your argument as far as I'm concerned. 

If Mr. Chusid is such an expert on pre-CD Jandek why isn't that evident from his writing? What makes him an expert? A few telephone conversations and letters? I would be a lot more impressed and convinced by a well articulated and intelligent analysis of Jandek's work. If you could point me to such a piece of writing by Mr. Chusid I would appreciate that very much, because I can't find it in his book. You keep talking about a difference of opinion, but there's more to it than that. If Chusid wants to be considered an "expert" he needs to provide more than his opinion. There has to be some meaningful insight, some degree of interpretation. My point is that his insights are not very informative or professional. Contrast them with the interviews in Jandek On Corwood.  I didn't agree with everything said in that interview, and not all of those people interviewed appeared to be Jandek fans, but all of them had intelligent things to say.  Chusid doesn't. He just wants to ridicule and insult Jandek and his work. That doesn't sound like the efforts of an "expert".

As far as talking about someone "through the lookingglass OF the music", I think that's just foolishness and bad analysis. Such techniques of interpretation are specious at best. The literary world abandoned them for much of the 20th century, (The New Criticism, for example, and Russian Formalism I believe). I remember reading an interview a number of years ago in which William Burroughs talked about how he was accused of antisemitism because one of his characters made antisemitic comments. The character was a Nazi, and Burroughs, (who wasn't an anti-Semite),  responded with "what else would you expect a Nazi to say?". The accusation came from just the sort of interpretation you indicate Chusid of engaging in. Can you see why it's hard to take seriously?

It might be fun to theorize or speculate on The Rep's personal life and its relationship to his music, but it's not the sort of serious analysis I'd expect from and "expert".  This isn't about me disagreeing with his opinions. I know a few people who really dislike Jandek's music and I respect their opinions because they are able to offer insight into why they think the way they do, beyond, "he's crazy" and "it sounds weird". Unfortunately our Jandek expert Mr. Chusid can't do nearly as much as these amateurs. I think I have expressed my point of view very clearly, and I certainly don't detect any sort of "straw-man" aspect to it. I think the only things you have pointed out is your willingness to blindly defend your friend, (who even you suspect is disingenuous with regards to his views on Jandek).  I don't think that Jandek, or Jandek fans owe Chusid anything. Jandek is a good story that it appears Mr. Chusid has enjoyed telling again and again. I'm sure it's served him well over the years.

Also, not everyone, (for example myself), is in America, and I'm not sure what nationality has to do with expressing oneself. Lot's of people outside of the USA enjoy

Well "Jesus Knievel", we will have to agree to disagree on Irwin's intent. I know Irwin professionally and personally and perhaps my insight comes from more than just reading the chapter in his book. He is who turned me and my friends on to Jandek many many years ago and I know that he turned a lot of people on to him. He knows the early records and is very much an expert on Jandek's pre-CD period whether you agree or not.  My feelings on it is that he was as drawn to them as equally as he was repelled by them. His attention and focus on Jandek shows he is maybe not being completely 100% honest in his appearance of disregarding Jandek or he wouldn't have spent the time or energy on him. I know this because there are loads of so called outsider 'geniuses' whose home-made cassettes and vanity pressed records have made their way in to Irwin's hands (he as you can expect is a magnet for such things) and he has assisted and decided they haven't the merit to focus or write about. 
Also, in music writing such as Irwin's on Jandek, the writer can talk about the maker of the music through the lookingglass OF the music and that is always how I read Irwin's words on Jandek. Back then, that is the only way you COULD talk about Jandek as he didn't make himself available beyond the music and the records. You are more than entitled to disagree with his take but to say he isn't an expert and doesn't know his subject just because you disagree with his opinions is just factually incorrect. But hey, this is America. Do what makes you feel good I guess.... but I too am free to point out the weaknesses in your straw-man arguments.

I do want to point out a difference for doing something 'just for the money' and writing a book that you sell. Jandek makes CDs that he sells, though I would never accuse him of doing it just for the money. See what I'm saying? I am sort of embarrassed I would have to point that out.
