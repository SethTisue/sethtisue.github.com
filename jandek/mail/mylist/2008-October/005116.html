Here's a couple of Jandek stories, preparing all for his appearance at The
Wexner Center in Columbus.

"To understand the complexity of this mysterious figure, you have to start
the story from the beginning. In 1978, an unknown record label in Houston,
known as Corwood Industries (which sounds suspiciously like some shady,
conspiracy-laden company from a movie that ends up developing zombies or
Terminators or something) began releasing the albums of the equally puzzling
artist known as Jandek."

http://uweekly.com/newsmag/10-08-2008/9361

"His small but loyal fan base is beyond geeked at the chance to see the
reclusive composer in the flesh, but most folks might wonder why his
upcoming performance here is so significant. After hearing his bizarre,
inaccessible music, they would probably be even more puzzled. To alleviate
some of the confusion, here's a quick primer on the mystery man from Houston
and his vast catalog of music."

http://tinyurl.com/48uc69

"Three decades ago, an album credited to the Units was released on the
obscure Corwood Industries label out of Houston. "Ready for the House"
sounded like the blues rattling around the head of a trauma survivor, a
lonely hellscape of Dadaist guitar strumming and otherworldly crooning from,
it appeared, the bottom of a well."

http://tinyurl.com/3jo5uk

the other day a friend/bandmate sent around a mass email to a bunch of
other friends/bandmates asking, "Hey, who wants to drive to Columbus
to see Jandek?!?"

this was the first I'd heard about his appearance there.

my immediate reply:

"You couldn't *pay* me enough to go see Jandek!"

my friend's reply to me:

"I've never heard Jandek's music"

my reply back to him:

"ohh -- that explains it.."

I know he's got his dedicated cult followers, but man - I find his
stuff completely unlistenable. Not only is it poorly and weakly
performed and bad-sounding, it's also just completely and utterly
*boring*, which is really the part that makes it unlistenable to me.
Because, really, I'm usually all *about* bad-sounding music..

Anyway -

I'm quite pleased because I'm going to be going to see a rare Akron
appearance by DEVO one week from today - they're doing a big DEVOBAMA
rally/fundraiser down at the Akron Civic Theatre. I have a feeling
they're really going to tear the place down!
