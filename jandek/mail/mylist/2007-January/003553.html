last.fm began as an extension of a program called audioscrobbler. 
basically users sign up so the site can log what they've been 
listening to on their windows media players.  pages get created for 
the artists either by fans or automatically, i'm not sure.  but then 
once fans find the page related to the artist they can choose to join 
it to show their appreciation.  this can all be done without any 
artist or label intervention or approval.

however, i think artists or labels have to upload songs in an 
official capacity for them to be fully available on the site.  once 
they are, users and the audioscrobbler program strive to come up with 
other rtists deemd "similar" and this creates links and also invents 
a streaming "radio station" based on the listener's tastes.  for 
example, typing in "jandek" to their "similar artist radio" i got 
islaja, gang gang dance, the red krayola, and no-neck blues band as 
the next 4 tracks.

there are a few tracks on the jandek page in preview, i'm not sure if 
they'd come up on a "station" and play in full.
